Main Description

An informal review of one engineer's work by another. Two sets of eyes see more than one, so the reviewer may spot mistakes that were missed initially. This is not an authoritative review, and is mostly intended to highlight unfounded assumptions and to stir up constructive discussion about the design decisions that were made.

Peer review sessions are also prime opportunities for coaching less experienced engineers. It is important that review comments are presented as constructive criticism, with respect for design decisions that were made.

An alternative could be a form of pair programming, where the peer review essentially takes place simultaneously with the work being done in the first place. This may or may not be practical, depending on the nature of the work.
