Task Descriptor: Review Project
After the project is completed, review the project to learn lessons that allow the organisation to improve and benefit.

Main Description

1. Introduction
Completing a project is not the same thing as ending the project management process. Simply finishing doesn't ensure that the organization benefits from the project's outcome. To make the most of the benefits that the project can deliver, however, you also need to check to see if further improvements will deliver still greater benefit.

It is also key to ensure that the lessons learned during the project are not forgotten. Design and execution can be more effectively, when taking advantage of lessons learned through experience of previous projects.

2. When to Review
A good time to start thinking about the Project Review is when members of the project team remember the most â€“ shortly after the project has been delivered, and when most of the problems have been resolved. Start to list ideas and observations while they are still fresh in people's minds.

However, to adequately assess the quality of the implementation and complete this process, it is preffered to  wait long enough for the changes caused by the project to truly take effect. There will probably be a period of adjustment before  the solution can be be reviewed as it was intended to operate.

3. What to Review
When conducting the review, a series of topics need to be addressed. The project charter needs to be evaluated to identify how closely the project results match the original objectives as well as the expected deliverables (including documentation) to ensure either that these have been delivered to an acceptable level of quality or that an acceptable substitute is in place.

Another important factor is the satisfaction of stakeholders. Meeting all objectives and deliverables will not always mean that the stakeholders are satisfied.

4. How to Review
Define the scope of the review and clarify the objectives for the review. This will ensure that people share their experiences openly and honestly.
Gather the key project documents. This will help to assess the project planning process, as well as the actual benefits achieved through the project. Where possible, use outside people in the review process to get an objective, unclouded view of the project. Sometimes it is recommended using only independent people in the review, however, the perspectives of those who were directly involved in the project can also provide insights.
Report the findings, and publicize the results. The Project review is designed to aid project managers conduct more effective projects in the future, as well as to measure and optimize the benefits of the specific project being reviewed.  This can be supported by presenting detailed recommendations to the organization and the project leaders, as well as to customers and other stakeholders.
