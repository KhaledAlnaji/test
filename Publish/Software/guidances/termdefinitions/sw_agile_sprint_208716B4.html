Term Definition: Sprint
Main Description

A sprint is a regular, repeatable work cycle during which work is completed and made ready for demonstration and release to the customer. It is a "time boxed" period during which the team performs a development cycle, resulting in a deliverable. Typically sprints are 2-6 weeks in duration.

All sprints are preceded by a  Sprint Planning Session where the sprint tasks are established and identified, and an estimated commitment of sprint goals is made. The product owner and team decide what needs to be moved from Product Backlog into Sprint Backlog.
During the sprint, teams check in at a daily stand-up meeting. Such meetings give the team a chance to update project status, discuss solutions and challenges, and broadcast their progress to product owners.

A sprint is followed by a Sprint Review Meeting that reviews and demonstrates de developed user stories and a Sprint Retrospective where the process is reviewed in order to identify lessons that can be used to improve the next sprint.
