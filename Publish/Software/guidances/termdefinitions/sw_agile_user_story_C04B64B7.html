Term Definition: User Story
Main Description

A user story is one or more sentences in the everyday or technical language of the team that captures what a Product Owner needs from the product that will be developed by the project. 
User stories are used as the basis for defining the functions the developed software must provide, and to facilitate requirements management. It captures the 'who', 'what' and 'why' of a requirement (or group of requirements) in a simple, concise way, often limited in detail by what can be hand-written on a small paper notecard.

User stories are written by or for the user/product owner as that person's primary way to influence the functionality of the software being developed. User stories may also be written by developers to express non-functional requirements (security, performance, quality, etc.), though primarily it is the task of a product owner to ensure user stories are captured.
