    Main Description

    The Scrum Team is a collection of Software- Developers, Testers, Architects and a Scrum Master whom together are responsible for the outcome of a Sprint or series of Sprints. This team is mentioned as a seperate role, because in SCRUM it is this team as a whole that is the main responsible for the outcome of a Sprint or project.

    Two defining characteristcs of a Scrum Team are "Self Empowered" and "Self Learning". Self Empowered means that it is the team as a whole that commits to the amount of work that can be perfomed in each sprint and as a result also is responsible for the outcome of the sprint. If a Sprint does not deliver the expected result during a sprint it is never considered to be an individual's fault, but always the responsibility of the entire team. The team as a whole is also allowed to (within the bounderies of the process and project) find its own best way to accomplish the work they have committed to.
    Self Learning refers to the goal that a Scrum Team should always strive to enhance the competences of all of its members at all times. This can be done by swapping roles, pair programming, on the job training or any other method that helps each member to be able to perform multiple roles.
