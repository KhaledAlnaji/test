Task: Sprint Retrospective
Session to determine which elements of the sprint needs to be retained and which elements need to be improved.
Disciplines: All Software Tasks, MAN.3 - Software Project Management
Purpose
The retrospective looks at how the team is working,  determining which elements of that needs to be retained and which elements need to be improved.
Relationships
RolesPrimary Performer:
  • Product Owner
  • Scrum Team
Additional Performers:
InputsMandatory:
  • Product Backlog
  • Product Burndown
  • Software Open Issue List
  • Sprint Review Report
    		Optional:
    • None
    Outputs
    • Software Open Issue List
      Main Description

      Scrum is not a prescriptive methodology but rather it is a framework that should be adapted appropriately for any given project, team and specific circumstances. The Sprint retrospective is an important mechanism that allows a team to continuously evolve and improve through the life of a project.

      It is important that everyone (including the Team, Product Owner and ScrumMaster) get a chance to air their opinion in an open, honest yet constructive atmosphere. It often helps to get an external facilitator in to help gain the maximum benefit from the retrospective; this approach has the additional benefit of freeing the ScrumMaster to participate more fully in the meeting. An impartial facilitator is particularly useful if emotions and relationships in the team are not as harmonious as they could be.

      Anything that affects how the team operates is open for debate, this might include: processes, practices, communication, environment, artefacts and tools.

      Steps
      Plan meeting
      Plan meeting and invite all stakeholders
      Facilitate the meeting
      Ensure that all attendees can openly discuss the strenghts and weaknesses of the team.
      Document outcome
      Ensure that the outcome of the meeting is documented and can be used for new sprints.
      Define improvements
      It is not always possible to adress all the outcome in the next sprint. Depending on the teamsize and / or the nature of the action points the team selects some of them as goal for the next sprint.
